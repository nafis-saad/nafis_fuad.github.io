## Overview

This project focuses on **flood risk assessment for Detroit** in collaboration with the Michigan Department of Environment, Great Lakes, and Energy (EGLE). Detroit faces significant flood challenges due to aging stormwater infrastructure, increasing extreme precipitation events, and urban heat island effects.

## Research Goals

- Identify flood-prone neighborhoods using spatial analysis
- Integrate LiDAR elevation data with hydrological models
- Develop risk maps to inform climate adaptation policy
- Support EGLE's environmental justice initiatives

## Methodology

- LiDAR and DEM-based terrain analysis
- Hydrological modeling of stormwater flow
- Machine learning-based flood prediction
- GIS visualization of risk zones

## Tools & Technologies

- **Python & Jupyter Notebook** — Analysis and modeling
- **ArcGIS / QGIS** — Geospatial processing
- **LiDAR data** — High-resolution terrain analysis
- **Hydrological modeling tools**
