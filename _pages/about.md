I am a Ph.D. student in **Civil and Transportation Engineering** at [Wayne State University](https://wayne.edu), based in Detroit, Michigan. My research sits at the intersection of artificial intelligence and urban infrastructure — using machine learning to understand, model, and improve how cities and people move.

## Research Interests

My primary research domains include:

- **Large Language Models (LLMs)** — Exploring how generative AI can be applied to civil engineering problems, from natural language querying of urban datasets to AI-assisted infrastructure decision support.
- **Geospatial Intelligence** — Leveraging GIS, remote sensing, and spatial machine learning to analyze terrain, climate patterns, and urban morphology. Applications include landslide susceptibility mapping and flood risk assessment.
- **Transportation Modeling** — Building four-step travel demand models for metropolitan regions using TransCAD, ArcGIS Pro, and Python. Current focus on the Sacramento Region and Great Lakes metro areas.
- **Computer Vision & Deep Learning** — Applying vision models to transportation infrastructure problems including road condition detection and satellite image analysis.

## Current Work

I am currently working on:
- Computer Vision applications for transportation infrastructure
- Deep learning architectures for geospatial prediction tasks
- Next-generation travel demand modeling frameworks

I am actively looking to **collaborate on machine learning research** with real-world impact. If you are working on related problems, feel free to reach out!
